The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, a prominent Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India, specifically within the Vishwanath Gali, and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The temple, also known as the Golden Temple, stands on the western bank of the holy Ganges River and is a significant pilgrimage site. Location: Situated in Varanasi, a city also known as Kashi, the temple is located on the western bank of the Ganges River.
